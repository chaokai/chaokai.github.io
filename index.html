About

Bridging theory, algorithms, and practical machine learning systems.

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University. My research develops learning algorithms for realistic settings where supervision is imperfect, labels may be noisy, environments may change over time, and feedback may be partial or delayed.

Before joining NYCU, I was a Project Assistant Professor in the Sugiyama-Yokoya-Ishida Laboratory at the University of Tokyo, a data scientist at Appier, and a postdoctoral scholar/fellow at USC, UCLA, and the University of Leoben. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from National Taiwan University.

Research

Research agenda

My long-term vision is to understand the fundamental limits of supervision and to design algorithms that remain effective when real-world data and feedback violate clean modeling assumptions.

Imperfect supervision

I study weakly supervised learning and learning with label noise, including unified risk analysis, robust imitation learning from vague feedback, reliable validation under noisy labels, and domain adaptation under distribution shift.

Online learning and bandits

I develop algorithms and regret analyses for online convex optimization and multi-armed bandits, including best-of-both-worlds bandits, Thompson sampling, Pareto front identification, and contextual/dueling bandits.

Adaptive learning systems

I am interested in turning theoretical insights into practical systems, including federated multi-task learning, budgeted hyper-parameter tuning, and LLM routing with preference or dueling feedback.
